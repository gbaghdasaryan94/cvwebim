Top 10 must-have skills

WHAT ARE THE TOP 10 SKILLS THAT'LL GET YOU A JOB WHEN YOU GRADUATE?

Commercial awarenessarrow_drop_down

This is about knowing how a business or industry works and what makes a company tick.

Showing that you have an understanding of what the organisation wants to achieve through its products and services, and how it competes in its marketplace.

Communicationsarrow_drop_down

This covers verbal and written communication, and listening.

It's about being clear, concise and focused; being able to tailor your message for the audience and listening to the views of others.

Teamworkarrow_drop_down

You'll need to prove that you're a team player but also have the ability to manage and delegate to others and take on responsibility.

It's about building positive working relationships that help everyone to achieve goals and business objectives.

Negotiation and persuasionarrow_drop_down

This is about being able to set out what you want to achieve and how, but also being able to understand where the other person is coming from so that you can both get what you want or need and feel positive about it.

Organisationarrow_drop_down

This is about showing that you can prioritise, work efficiently and productively, and manage your time well.

It's also good to be able to show employers how you decide what is important to focus on and get done, and how you go about meeting deadlines.

Problem solvingarrow_drop_down

You need to display an ability to take a logical and analytical approach to solving problems and resolving issues.

It's also good to show that you can approach problems from different angles.

Leadershiparrow_drop_down

You may not be a manager straight away, but graduates need to show potential to motivate teams and other colleagues that may work for them.

It's about assigning and delegating tasks well, setting deadlines and leading by good example.

Perseverance and motivationarrow_drop_down

Employers want people to have a bit of get-up-and-go.

Working life presents many challenges and you need to show employers that you're the kind of person who will find a way through, even when the going gets tough... and stay cheerful-ish.

Ability to work under pressurearrow_drop_down

This is about keeping calm in a crisis and not becoming too overwhelmed or stressed.

It involves dealing with constraints which are often outside of your control - these might be resource or time constraints, the difficulty of the task or having insufficient knowledge required to complete the task, or unforeseen changes or problems.

Confidencearrow_drop_down

In the workplace you need to strike the balance of being confident in yourself but not arrogant, but also have confidence in your colleagues and the company you work for.

Self-confidence is extremely important in almost every aspect of our lives, yet so many people struggle to find it. Sadly, this can be a vicious circle: people who lack self-confidence can find it difficult to become successful.
